Certificates

Issue, manage, and distribute blockchain-backed certificates.

Certificates Page Overview

The Certificates page is the main hub of the platform. It has three tabs:

  • Create — Issue new certificates (single or batch).
  • Issued — View, search, and manage all issued certificates.
  • Stats — Analytics dashboard with issuance metrics.

Issuing a Single Certificate

  1. Go to the Certificates page and select the Create tab.
  2. Fill in the required fields:
    • Name — The name of the certified company or individual.
    • Competency — The qualification or certification type.
    • Expiry Date — When the certificate expires.
  3. Optionally add:
    • Company Email — Recipient's email for certificate delivery.
    • Certificate Image — Upload an image to embed in the certificate.
  4. Click Add Certificate to mint it on the blockchain.
Blockchain minting When you issue a certificate, it is minted as an ERC-721 NFT on the blockchain. This makes it tamper-proof and independently verifiable by anyone.

Batch Issuance (CSV)

You can issue up to 25 certificates at once by uploading a CSV file. For detailed instructions on preparing your CSV, see the Batch Upload Guide.

Managing Issued Certificates

On the Issued tab, you can see all certificates with their status and perform actions:

Individual Actions

  • Email — Send the certificate with its QR code to the recipient's email address.
  • Revoke — Permanently revoke a certificate. This updates its on-chain status.
  • Extend — Extend the expiry date of a certificate.
  • Download QR — Download the branded QR code image for the certificate.

Bulk Actions

  • Email All — Send certificates to all recipients who have a company email.
  • Bulk Renew — Extend expiry for multiple certificates at once.
  • Export CSV — Download a CSV of all issued certificates.

Certificate Statuses

StatusMeaning
ValidCertificate is active and has not expired.
ExpiredCertificate has passed its expiry date. It can be extended.
RevokedCertificate has been permanently revoked. It cannot be reinstated.

Emailing Certificates

When you email a certificate, the recipient receives a branded email containing their certificate details, a QR code image, and a link to view and verify the certificate online. The email includes:

  • Welcome message from your organisation.
  • Instructions for displaying the Registered Member certificate.
  • The QR code image (can be scanned to verify).
  • Certificate details: company name, competency, expiry date.
  • A direct link to view and verify the certificate.
Important You cannot email a revoked certificate. The recipient's companyEmail field must be set before you can send.

Expiry Notifications

The platform automatically sends email notifications to certificate holders as their certificates approach expiry. Notifications are sent:

  • 30 days before expiry
  • 7 days before expiry
  • 1 day before expiry
  • 7 days after expiry

Stats Dashboard

The Stats tab shows analytics about your certificate issuance, including the number of certificates issued, their statuses, and trends over time.
