Certificate Verification

Verify the authenticity of any Sigalmark certificate.

Overview

Every Sigalmark certificate is minted as an NFT on the blockchain. This means anyone can independently verify that a certificate is authentic, has not been tampered with, and is still valid — without needing to contact the issuing organisation.

Verification Methods

1. Paste a Link (Primary Method)

The simplest way to verify a certificate:

  1. Go to the Verify page on the Sigalmark website (no login required).
  2. Paste a certificate link into the input field. This can be:
    • A short URL (e.g. sm1.it/AbCd) — found on certificate QR codes.
    • A full verification URL with contract address parameters.
  3. Click Verify. The system resolves the link and fetches the certificate data from the blockchain.
Tip You can scan a certificate QR code with your phone's camera app. It will open the short URL in your browser, where you can verify it directly.

2. Manual Lookup (Advanced)

If you have the raw blockchain details of a certificate:

  1. On the Verify page, click Manual lookup (advanced) to expand the form.
  2. Enter the Contract Address, Token ID, and Network ID.
  3. Click Verify.

3. Scan the QR Code (Mobile App)

For on-the-go verification:

  1. Open the Sigalmark mobile app on your phone.
  2. Tap Start Scanning and point your camera at the certificate's QR code.
  3. The app will verify the certificate and show a pass/fail result with details.

Verification Results

After verification, results are displayed in several sections:

Verdict Badge

A large coloured banner at the top shows the overall result:

VerdictColourMeaning
VALIDGreenThe certificate is authentic, current, and not revoked.
INVALIDRedThe certificate has issues — it may be revoked, expired, or not found.
UNKNOWNGreyThe certificate could not be verified (e.g. unrecognised URL or network error).

Reason Code Pills

Below the verdict, small coloured pills explain why the verdict was reached. Examples:

  • Short URL authorised — the short link was resolved successfully.
  • Certificate found — the certificate exists on the blockchain.
  • Not expired / Not revoked — the certificate is still current.
  • Certificate revoked / Certificate expired — issues found.

Certificate Details Card

Shows the full certificate information:

  • Name — The certified company or individual.
  • Competency — The qualification or certification type.
  • Issued by — The issuing organisation.
  • Issued / Expiry — Certificate dates.
  • Status — Valid, Expired, or Revoked.

Technical Details

A collapsible section shows the blockchain details: contract address, token ID, network ID, and a link to the blockchain explorer where the NFT can be inspected independently.

Branded Verification Pages

Each organisation has a branded verification page at its own URL (e.g. sigalmark.com/your-org/contract-address). When someone scans a QR code or follows a certificate link, they see the verification result on a page branded with the issuing organisation's logo and colours.

Mobile App

The Sigalmark mobile app (available for iOS and Android) provides camera-based QR scanning optimised for certificate verification. Features include:

  • Real-time camera scanning with auto-verification.
  • Focus and zoom controls for scanning printed certificates.
  • Pass/fail result with full certificate details.
  • Works offline for scanning — blockchain verification requires an internet connection.
